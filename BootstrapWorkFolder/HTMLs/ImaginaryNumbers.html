Imaginary Numbers and the Complex Plane

Imaginary and Complex Numbers

The idea of imaginary numbers stems from the imaginary unit, \(i\). This unit is shown as the following number with the equivalent properties: \[i=\sqrt{-1}\] \[i^2=-1\] \[i^4=1\]

Complex numbers are any numbers that can be written in the form \(a+bi\) where \(a\) is a real number called the real part, and \(b\) is also a real number, but is called the imaginary part of the complex number.

The Complex Plane

The complex plane is a useful way to visualize the set of all complex numbers as it consists of two axes, one for the real part and one for the imaginary part.
