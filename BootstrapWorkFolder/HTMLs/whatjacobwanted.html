Our Motivation

The matter of representing an object’s orientation in space is one of importance in many industries and can involve some complex mathematical concepts that may be new to most. The simplest way to think of representing the orientation of an object is by what are known as Euler Angles, values that express the rotation around one of three axes in degrees. However, some problems can arise when relying on Euler angles solely to represent an object’s orientation in 3D space. A flaw known as gimbal lock occurs when two axes align and take away a degree of freedom. This problem is large enough to call for the use of another way to represent orientation, specifically when it comes to making various calculations. This is where quaternions are useful. Quaternions are made up of four numbers and are computationally more efficient, though conceptually more difficult to understand. They are widely used in several industries, so it is helpful to have a basic understanding of their role in 3D rotation.

A few specific industries for which 3D rotation and quaternions are applicable:

  • Computer Vision
  • Computer Graphics
  • Robotics
  • Flight Dynamics
  • Molecular Dynamics
